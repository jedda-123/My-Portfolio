About Me

Hello! My Name is Daniel Caragay

As a second-year BSIT student at Cavite State University – Imus Campus, I am gradually building a solid and broad foundation in the field of Information Technology, a foundation that serves as a bridge between technical infrastructure and cutting-edge software development. My journey into technology began in senior high school, where I honed my skills in system assembly, network configuration, and hardware troubleshooting. These experiences have given me a thorough understanding of how computer systems work from the simplest to the more complex levels.

Currently, I am focused on expanding my knowledge in programming and web development. I am proficient in languages such as Java, Python, and C++, and I am also comfortable working in Linux based environments, particularly Ubuntu. Beyond basic coding, I enjoy combining logic and design by using HTML, CSS, and PHP to create systems that are not only functional but also have a clear visual identity. In addition, my skills in digital editing help to improve the presentation and overall quality of my projects.

For me, Information Technology is a field that is constantly evolving and challenges the abilities of every individual. I believe that true progress depends on continuous learning, discipline, and the ability to adapt to the rapid changes in technology. I aspire to be a skilled and analytical IT professional who can tackle complex technical problems while also creating creative digital solutions with confidence and professionalism.
